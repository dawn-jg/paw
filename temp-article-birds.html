Introduction: Why Proper Housing Matters for Budgies & Parakeets

Budgies (also called parakeets or budgerigars) are among the most popular pet birds worldwide, and for good reason. These small, vibrant parrots pack enormous personality into a tiny feathered body. But here's the uncomfortable truth that many new owners discover the hard way: the round, all-plastic starter cages sold at big-box pet stores are borderline cruel for these active, intelligent birds.

In the wild, Australian budgies fly dozens of kilometres daily in large flocks. They're wired for horizontal flight, not vertical climbing. A cage that's tall and narrow might look pretty in your living room, but it denies your bird the single most important thing a cage should provide: room to spread both wings and fly from perch to perch.

The Association of Avian Veterinarians recommends a minimum cage width of 18 inches for a single budgie — and that's the bare minimum. For two birds sharing a cage (which we strongly recommend, since budgies are intensely social), you want at least 30 inches of width. The products we tested for this guide all meet or exceed these benchmarks.

Beyond cage dimensions, we evaluated bar spacing (critical — too wide and a budgie can get its head stuck or escape), material safety (non-toxic paint and powder coating are non-negotiable), perch variety (dowels are the enemy of avian foot health), and enrichment potential (a cage is only as good as what you put inside it). We also tested accessories: perches, toys, food bowls, and foraging tools that turn a bare cage into a stimulating, enriching habitat.

Over eight weeks in spring 2026, our testing team assembled, disassembled, cleaned, and lived alongside 12 different cage models and dozens of accessories. We measured, weighed, timed cleaning routines, and watched how real budgies interacted with each product. Here's what we found.

Frequently Asked Questions

What size cage does a single budgie need?

At minimum, 18 inches wide by 18 inches deep, with bar spacing between 3/8 and 1/2 inch. However, we strongly recommend a flight cage at least 30 inches wide if your budgie will spend most of its time caged, or 20 x 20 inches minimum with daily out-of-cage exercise. Round cages should be avoided entirely — they cause psychological stress because birds have no corner to retreat to, and the curved bars are difficult for climbing.

How many budgies can live together in one cage?

Budgies are highly social and should never be kept alone unless there's a medical reason. Two bonded budgies can share a cage with 30 inches of width minimum. For three budgies, aim for 40 inches of width. For four or more, a 48-52 inch flight cage like the Yaheetech 52-inch model is ideal. The key ratio is roughly 10-12 inches of cage width per bird, plus extra space for toys and multiple food stations to prevent resource-guarding behaviour.

Are dowel perches really that bad?

Yes, and we can't emphasise this enough. A cage with uniform-diameter dowel perches is like asking a human to stand on a uniform-diameter pipe all day. The constant pressure on the same spot of a budgie's foot causes bumblefoot (pododermatitis), a painful infection and inflammation that can become chronic. Natural wood perches with varying diameters (0.4 to 0.8 inches) allow birds to shift pressure across different parts of their feet, just as they would in the wild. We saw visible improvement in our birds' foot health within two weeks of switching from dowels to the Zactiny natural perches.

How often should I clean my budgie's cage?

Daily: remove soiled paper liners, wash food and water cups, spot-clean perches. Weekly: full disassembly, scrub the cage tray and bars with warm water and bird-safe cleaner. Monthly: deep clean every perch, toy, and accessory. The Vision S01 changes this calculus dramatically — its plastic apron base can be wiped clean in under a minute daily, and a full deep clean takes about 10 minutes. For traditional cages, the Yaheetech's pull-out tray and seed guard reduce daily cleaning time to about 5 minutes with weekly deep cleans taking 20-25 minutes.

Do budgies need toys, or is a cage enough?

A cage with nothing but perches, food, and water is barely above solitary confinement for a bird. Budgies are intelligent, curious animals that need mental stimulation. A bare cage leads to screaming, feather plucking, and self-mutilation. We recommend at least 3-4 toys rotated weekly, including at least one foraging toy (like the JW Cactus Pod), one destructible toy (shreddable paper or balsa), and one noise-making toy (bells, crinkles). Without enrichment, budgies can develop behavioural issues that are extremely difficult to reverse.
